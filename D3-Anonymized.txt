EXECUTIVE FINDING

[FINTECH-CLIENT] was compromised in Q2 2024 by a known threat actor named The Griot. Between June 3-5, The Griot conducted a targeted attack that resulted in the theft of customer financial data (names, addresses, transaction amounts). The attack succeeded not because of a technical weakness but because [FINTECH-CLIENT]'s security team dismissed every alert that detected it.

What happened:
On June 4 at 02:07 UTC, The Griot broke into the account of [OPERATIONS-ANALYST] using valid credentials. Within 54 minutes, The Griot created a compressed file containing [FINTECH-CLIENT]'s customer and transaction databases and exfiltrated it to an external server. The SIEM flagged this activity as CRITICAL severity but the security analyst dismissed it as "probably nothing."

Who is affected:
Every customer whose data appears in customers.csv and transactions.csv is now at risk. [FINTECH-CLIENT]'s customer trust is compromised, and regulatory bodies (Data Protection Officer, financial authorities) will require notification.

What we don't know:
- How The Griot obtained [OPERATIONS-ANALYST]'s credentials (phishing, credential stuffing, insider threat, password spray)
- Whether The Griot accessed additional data beyond customers.csv and transactions.csv
- Whether The Griot installed persistence mechanisms (backdoors, scheduled tasks) that remain active
- Whether additional user accounts were compromised beyond [OPERATIONS-ANALYST] and [FORMER-EMPLOYEE-ACCOUNT]
- Whether The Griot is still in the system

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHAT IS AT RISK

1. Data at Risk:
The stolen databases contain customer names, addresses and transaction records. This is regulated data under the Nigerian Data Protection Regulation (NDPR). Each customer record is personally identifiable information (PII) and [FINTECH-CLIENT] is legally obligated to notify affected individuals of the breach, document it with the [DPO], and conduct impact assessments.

2. Threat Actor Profile:
The Griot is a known regional threat actor documented in threat-intel feeds. This is not a random attacker or opportunistic breach. It is a targeted, sophisticated campaign that used valid credentials (either obtained through credential theft or purchase from underground markets) and the attacker demonstrated knowledge of [FINTECH-CLIENT]'s systems (knew which databases to target, understood file paths and compression techniques) and operated with quite a deliberate intent.

3. System Persistence Risk:
On June 4 at 02:14 UTC, a cron RELOAD event occurred outside normal maintenance windows. This indicates that The Griot could have installed persistence mechanisms (backdoors, scheduled tasks, reverse shells) and if it was successful, then The Griot may still have access to [FINTECH-CLIENT]'s systems even after credential reset.

4. Secondary Account Compromise:
Account [FORMER-EMPLOYEE-ACCOUNT] (offboarded April 12) appears in authentication logs on June 3, 4, 5. Either [FINTECH-CLIENT] failed to deactivate [FORMER-EMPLOYEE-ACCOUNT]'s account (process failure) or The Griot also obtained [FORMER-EMPLOYEE-ACCOUNT]'s credentials. Either scenario indicates a second potential entry point into the system.

SYSTEMIC RECOMMENDATION

[FINTECH-CLIENT] must implement a mandatory escalation rule: CRITICAL and HIGH severity alerts require peer review and manager approval before closure. This is a non-negotiable policy change.
